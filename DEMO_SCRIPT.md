# Handy — Demo Video Script

**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover (you talking naturally)
**Tone:** Casual, relatable, a little funny — like explaining it to a friend
**Length:** ~2.5 to 3 minutes
**Language:** Simple, natural Indian English — the way you'd actually say it

---

## COLD OPEN — The Problem (0:00 – 0:35)

> **SCREEN:** Screen recording of Blender open, with a weird half-modeled alien (Rocky). You're clearly stuck.

**YOU (voiceover):**

"So I was trying to model this alien — Rocky, from Project Hail Mary — in Blender. Beautiful movie, terrible idea to DIY the 3D model.

I had no clue what I was doing."

> **SCREEN:** Cut to — you taking a screenshot of Blender, switching to a browser tab with Gemini/ChatGPT, uploading the screenshot, waiting for the response, reading the response, switching back to Blender, hunting for the button it mentioned, not finding it, going back to Gemini to ask "where exactly is the subdivision surface modifier?"... repeat.

**YOU:**

"This was my workflow. Screenshot. Upload. Wait. Read. Go back. Hunt for the button. Can't find it. Go back again. Ask again. And again. And again.

For EVERY. SINGLE. STEP."

> **SCREEN:** Quick montage of 4-5 tab switches, each one faster, building frustration. Maybe a dramatic zoom into the Chrome tab count.

**YOU:**

"Same thing happened when I was trying to set up an SDK in Xcode. I don't know Xcode. I don't know where 'Add Package Dependency' is. I don't know what a build scheme is. Every click was a round trip to the AI chatbot."

> **BEAT.** Brief pause.

**YOU:**

"It hit me — the AI knows what to do. I know what I want. But the gap between us? Alt-Tab."

## DEMO BLOCK 1 — Voice + Screen Awareness (0:55 – 1:25)

> **SCREEN:** Open Blender with the Rocky model. Press Control+Z (push-to-talk). The companion cursor's waveform bars start animating — you're speaking.

**YOU (into mic, naturally):**

"Hey Handy, I want to add eyes to this character. Where do I start?"

> **SCREEN:** Handy captures your screen, the blue companion cursor shows a processing spinner, then a short spoken response appears near the cursor. The blue cursor FLIES — smooth Bezier arc animation — to the exact button in Blender's UI and points at it with a label like "Add > Mesh > UV Sphere."

**YOU (voiceover):**

"It looked at my screen. Understood where I am. And literally pointed at the button."

> **SCREEN:** The chat panel slides in from the right showing the full detailed response — step by step instructions, properly formatted.

**YOU:**

"And if that one-liner wasn't enough — full detailed answer is right here in the chat. Voice for speed. Chat for depth."

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Recording Checklist
- Clean desktop, dark mode, minimal distractions
- Have Blender open with the Rocky model (or any 3D project) as the primary demo context
- Have a few browser tabs ready: GitHub, LinkedIn, and one more
- Terminal open with some code/project visible
- Sarvam API credits loaded for the TTS demo moment

### Pacing Tips
- The cold open should feel slightly frustrated and real — don't over-act, just be yourself
- The "EVERY. SINGLE. STEP." line should have actual pauses between words
- The reveal moment ("So I built Handy") should be calm and confident — not hyped
- Demo blocks should be flowing screen recordings, not cuts — show the real speed
- Let the companion cursor's Bezier animation do the talking — it's visually stunning, don't narrate over it

### Audio
- Record voiceover separately for cleaner audio (you can re-record lines)
- BUT the voice input demo (Block 1) should be live/real — that authenticity sells it
- Use a decent mic, even phone voice memos in a quiet room works

### Things That Demo Themselves (just show, don't explain)
- The smooth cursor flight animation (it's buttery — people will notice)
- The automatic context switch in the header bar (instant, no loading)
- The waveform animation on the widget when listening
- The streaming response appearing word by word in chat

### Suggested B-Roll / Quick Cuts
- The tab-switching montage in the cold open (3-4 seconds, fast cuts)
- A quick Settings view flash (don't linger, just establish it exists)
- The Finder window showing local JSON files (2 seconds max)

### Text Cards (if you want them)
- "Voice + Chat + Screen Awareness" — when showing Block 1
- "Contextual History Per App" — when showing Block 2
- "100% Local Storage" — during the privacy block
- "Powered by Sarvam AI" — during the TTS moment
